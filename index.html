About Me

Academic background, scholarly focus, and research orientation.

I am a final-year Bachelor of Science student in Physics at St. Xavier's College, Kathmandu, affiliated with Tribhuvan University. My academic focus is situated at the intersection of Computational Physics and Biophysics, with a particular emphasis on first-principles electronic structure calculations and the numerical modelling of complex quantum systems.

My research methodology employs density functional theory (DFT) frameworks — principally Quantum ESPRESSO and VASP — to investigate the structural, electronic, and mechanical properties of material systems ranging from transition-metal alloys to two-dimensional nanomaterials. I additionally engage in the numerical simulation of time-dependent quantum mechanical phenomena, including wave-packet dynamics and quantum tunnelling in non-trivial potential landscapes.

My broader scholarly interests encompass computational neuroscience, biophysical modelling, and the application of machine-learning techniques to physical simulation workflows. I am committed to open science, interdisciplinary collaboration, and contributing to the international physics research community.

Research Projects

Selected academic and independent research investigations encompassing computational material science, quantum mechanics, and biophysics.

Python Neural Networks Neuroscience

Neural Network Modelling in Computational Neuroscience

Led a team to design and implement a Python-based neural network simulating fundamental neuron interaction dynamics. Served as an introduction to computational neuroscience, demonstrating the application of machine-learning architectures to biological system modelling.

Quantum ESPRESSO DFT Biophysics

Molecular Analysis via Density Functional Theory

Conducted DFT-based electronic structure calculations to systematically analyse the structural and electronic properties of selected biomolecules, employing plane-wave pseudopotential methods within the Quantum ESPRESSO framework.

Quantum ESPRESSO Alloy Physics Material Science

Electronic Properties of CuNiPt Ternary Alloys

Computed band structure, projected density of states, and equilibrium lattice constants of CuNiPt ternary alloys. Findings contributed to the characterisation of alloy stability and electronic behaviour in multi-component transition-metal systems.

MATLAB Quantum Mechanics Numerical Methods

Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation — Double Potential Barrier

Numerically solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation to characterise quantum tunnelling, resonance transmission coefficients, and Gaussian wave-packet dynamics within a symmetric double-barrier potential configuration using finite-difference methods.

VASP Nanotechnology Graphene

Mechanical Characterisation of Graphene via Ab Initio Simulation

Led computational studies examining the mechanical properties of graphene-based material systems using VASP. Investigated stress-strain relationships and elastic constants as indicators of viability for nanoelectromechanical system (NEMS) applications.

Python Automation Reproducibility

Automated Computational Routines for Scientific Simulation

Developed a library of reusable Python routines for numerical modelling, data post-processing, and simulation workflow automation. Emphasis placed on reproducibility, modularity, and cross-platform compatibility across research projects.
